Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about Acetowhite Vision AI and how it works

What is Acetowhite Vision AI?
Acetowhite Vision AI is an artificial intelligence-powered screening tool designed to assist healthcare professionals in the early detection of cervical cancer. It analyzes cervical images to identify acetowhite changes that may indicate precancerous or cancerous lesions.
How accurate is the AI model?
Our AI model has been trained on a large dataset of cervical images and achieves high sensitivity to minimize false negatives. However, it should be used as a screening tool to support, not replace, clinical judgment by qualified healthcare professionals.
What is Grad-CAM and why is it important?
Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping) is an explainability technique that shows which regions of the image the AI focused on when making its prediction. This transparency helps clinicians understand and validate the AI's decision-making process.
Can I use this tool for diagnosis?
No, this tool is designed for screening purposes only. Any positive findings should be confirmed through proper clinical examination, colposcopy, and biopsy by a qualified healthcare professional. The AI assists in identifying cases that require further investigation.
What does the uncertainty score mean?
The uncertainty score indicates how confident the model is in its prediction. A high uncertainty score suggests the model is less certain about its prediction, which may warrant additional expert review or follow-up testing.
What image formats are supported?
The system supports common image formats including JPEG, PNG, and other standard medical imaging formats. Images should be clear, well-lit cervical photographs taken after acetic acid application.
Is patient data secure?
Yes, we take data security seriously. All images are processed securely and are not stored permanently unless explicitly saved by authorized users. The system complies with healthcare data protection standards.
Who should use this tool?
This tool is designed for use by healthcare professionals, including gynecologists, nurses, and trained health workers involved in cervical cancer screening programs, particularly in resource-limited settings where access to expert colposcopists may be limited.
